Wonder Theatre Redesign

Wonder Theatre is a nonprofit, live performance theatre engaging the San Antonio community with musical productions and educational programming year round.

This project redesigned the Wonder Theatre website to match its present day branding and increase user efficiency while navigating throughout the site.

The Problem

We believe updating the current website to include a more user-friendly navigation for those who enjoy the world of theater will achieve more growth and sustainability for performing arts and education in San Antonio.

How might we make users feel more confident and equipped with the proper knowledge about their local community theater show dates, times, contributions, and audition/volunteer resources?

The Solution

Create a responsive web design and improve user knowledge while gaining more traction about current events and shows.

The Research

Stakeholder Interview

We were fortunate enough to get an interview with a member from the Board of Directors. During the interview, we learned there would be a rebranding of the Woodlawn Theatre to Wonder Theater due to location change of venue. We also gained insight on the importance of donations and stakeholder’s desire of stronger call to action for donations.

User Interviews

For our User Interview Testing Plan, we interviewed 4 individuals that are part of or enjoy the world of performing arts. We had 18 participants take our survey with our main objective on testing the navigation bar feature and volunteer sign up forms. Through our user interviews and survey data, we found that:

  • Users prefer to buy tickets online
  • Users would use the sight to learn more information about upcoming shows
  • Users love to see shows multiple times a year
  • Users are passionate about the performing arts and are looking for ways to give back

User Persona

User Insight

A performing arts educator needs a seamless way to view important information about shows, youth programs, and auditions in order to accomplish her goal of sharing, advocating and promoting theater arts within her local community.

Storyboard

Our storyboard was created as a branch off of our User Persona, Audrey. We wanted to include the perspective of one her students, and highlight the importance of what new experiences can create.

Exploring Solutions

Feature Prioritization

We came to develop a possible solution by creating our I like, I wish, what if chart. The Feature Prioritization Matrix taught us that the most popular feature is the call to action for volunteers/donations and creating a visual calendar in which someone could see additional showdates.

Card Sorting/Sitemap

To come up with our sitemap we used the card sorting method. We took all of the pages on their current website and went through organizing, grouping and prioritizing until we came up with a user friendly navigation system. Previously they had a split navigation with items taking up space in the main navigation that we felt errr lower priority.

Low Fidelity Usability Testing

Before creating our mid fidelity wireframe, we ran usability tests to ensure our users could navigate throughout our designed pages. We tested 4 users and gave them the following tasks to complete:

  • Task 1: Access the get involved page
  • Task 2: Reach the volunteer form
  • Task 3: Submit a volunteer form
Through our low fidelity testing we saw that users generally wanted to know more about the available volunteer positions before signing up. A key insight we found was to make the form with with multi answer capability so users wouldn’t have to submit multiple times.

A/B Testing

We then implemented an A/B test to compare different calendars we wanted to include on our homepage. Through testing we found that users liked version b. Ultimately it performed better, and the difference was significant. Because of that we were fairly confident that it is preferred more, and not performing better due to random chance. Users gravitated towards b because of the drop down menu which allows for an easier date search than the arrows on version A.
